Overview

This module will give students the opportunity to think creatively about user experience and interaction design. Central to the module will be learning to apply a range of tools, techniques and methods. Through short lectures, and hands-on tutorials and practicals, you’ll be encouraged to foster creative impulses and open up spaces for creativity in design.

Throughout the module, you will be responding to a "design brief". The brief will invite opportunities to explore and design a proposal or intervention for the "Smart City". You’ll work in groups of 4-5 students over the term to both learn a range of design techniques and methods, and use these in your design practice.

Design Brief: Re-imagining the Smart City

The challenge will be to imaginatively combine a range of tools, techniques and methods to respond creatively to the design brief and think critically and reflectively about your practice.

Goals/Learning Outcomes

There will be five key areas of learning:

1. Core skills
Developing core skills using a range of creative tools, techniques and methods, including ethnographic observation, sketching, video, Arduino, etc.

2. Creative impulse
Developing a creative impulse for combining these skills to think witha design ‘problem’ and to see things differently, so as to imagine new and alternative forms of interaction and experience.

3. Collaborative design
Understanding the dynamics of creative group work and learning how to use collaboration to approach design and innovation.

4. Reflective practice
Building and refining your individual reflective practice in order to question and think critically about your work.

5. The Smart City
Understanding multiple perspectives onto the Smart City, including the lived experience of the city, monitoring and dashboards (e.g. energy, population, transport, climate), infrastructure, finance and debt, and accessibility.

Assessment

The module will use work on a group project and an individual “workbook” and blog page to assess participation and learning. Both forms of assessment will be worth 50% of the final overall mark for the module.

Group project (50%)

Students will be assessed on the progress they make in their group work over the term, and the quality of their group's final design proposal/intervention. Progress will be tracked in seminars and tutorials, and during an interim “crit” (Lecture 6). Each groups’ completed proposal/intervention will be judged in a final “crit” (Lecture 10) by the two lecturers and a guest. The group work will be assessed against:

i. its use of the tools, techniques and methods learnt on the module;
ii. the demonstration of productive and creative group work; and
iii. and what is judged to be the overall quality of the work.

Students in each group will all receive the same mark for the group work unless there is a clear indication of differences in individual contributions.

Individual workbook (50%)

Over the duration of the module, students will keep a paper-based "workbook" and/or a blog page logging their own contributions to their group project. Students will be introduced to the idea of a workbook for collecting and annotating sources of inspirations, ideas, etc. and asked to use and develop one of their own during the project. The workbook will be a blog*, but students will also be encouraged to compliment their online materials with a paper-based sketchbook.

This individual component of the module will invite students to think creatively about recording their work, and assess students on their ability to critically reflect on their ideas and progress. The individual work will be assessed against:

i. the demonstration of progression in thinking and design over the term;
ii. the ability to critically reflect on both individual and group work;
iii. the quality of the blog and/or workbook.

* There are many ways to keep a blog. See Wordpress and Tumblr. Also services like Pinterest and Medium can be adapted.
