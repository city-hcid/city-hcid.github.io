PhD Programme

At the Centre for HCID, we encourage a diversity of PhD research related to interactive systems, interaction design, and the study of sociotechnical domains. We pride ourselves on creating a supportive culture for post graduate research that is committed to both individual and collective development.

HCID staff members are happy to correspond and talk about their research interests, and offer advice on how to apply for a PhD. For further information about potential PhD supervisors, see our list of academic staff here.

  • Linda Berube

    Supervisors: Ernesto Priego, Stephann Makri

    Linda is an AHRC Collaborative Doctoral Programme student, working with The British Library. Her research is based on a mixed-methods and interdisciplinary approach to understanding the UK digital comics creation-to-consumption cycle.

  • Beatrice Vincenzi

    Supervisors: Alex Taylor, Simone Stumpf

    Beatrice is a 3 year PhD student and her research explores AI assistive technology for people with visual impairment to augment sighted guiding partnership and social interaction in navigation.

  • Srravya Chandhiramowuli

    Supervisors: Alex Taylor, Sara Heitlinger

    Srravya is a PhD student at HCID. Her research interests lie in examining the implications of computing technologies (including apps, data, algorithms and more) for labour processes and work practices.
