PhD studentship available for research in human-centric ethical AI development

Application deadline 30 April 2021

We are looking for an outstanding candidate for a doctoral studentship, starting October 2021, co-funded by City, University of London and Fujitsu Limited.

The project will be supervised by Dr Simone Stumpf and draw on her research expertise in end-user interactions with AI systems. The topic of PhD research will be at the intersection of HCI and AI, and focus on developing human-centric methods, techniques and interfaces for integrating ethics into the AI development lifecycle to ensure the autonomy of marginalised users and the fairness of AI algorithms.

Studentships provide an annual bursary of £16,000 and full tuition fees for Home students. Additional resources to cover tuition fees can be made available for outstanding Overseas candidates.

How to apply

Applications should demonstrate outstanding academic achievements and the potential to produce cutting-edge research. We are looking for a candidate with a good computing-related BSc, and a MSc degree in HCI or related area. Familiarity with AI concepts is expected. As part of the application, you will need to include a research proposal and Dr Stumpf is happy to discuss this prior to submission. For full details on how to apply please see here.
